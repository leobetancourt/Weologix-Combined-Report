LEADERSHIP

1. COACHING VS. SELF-IMPROVEMENT

Coaching: The tendency to effectively facilitate the development of others (one to one interactions). Self-improvement: The tendency to attempt to develop or better oneself.


2. PROVIDES DIRECTION VS. RECEIVES CORRECTION

Provides Direction: The tendency to provide others with clear direction or guidance. Receives Correction: The tendency to accept guidance intended to improve performance.


1

LEADERSHIP

3. AUTHORITATIVE VS. COLLABORATIVE

Authoritative: The desire for decision-making authority and the willingness to accept decision-making responsibility. Collaborative: The tendency to collaborate with others when making decisions.


4. HANDLES AUTONOMY VS. ORGANIZATIONAL COMPATIBILITY

Handles Autonomy: The tendency to have the motivation and self-reliance necessary for a significant amount of independence from immediate supervision. Organizational Compatibility: The tendency to work co-operatively with others (assuming sufficient job related knowledge and team compatibility).


2

LEADERSHIP

5. FLEXIBLE VS. PERSISTENT

Flexible: The tendency to easily adapt to change. Persistent: The tendency to be tenacious despite encountering significant obstacles.


6. OPEN / REFLECTIVE VS. CERTAIN

Open / reflective: The tendency to reflect on many different viewpoints. Certain: The tendency to feel confident in one's opinions.


3

LEADERSHIP

7. SELF-MOTIVATED VS. CAUSE MOTIVATED

Self-motivated: The drive to achieve including taking initiative, wanting challenge, and being enthusiastic about goals. Cause Motivated: The tendency to be motivated to help society.


8. WANTS TO LEAD VS. ENLISTS COOPERATION

Wants To Lead: The desire to be in a position to direct or guide others. Enlists Cooperation: The tendency to invite others to participate in or join an effort.


4

PROBLEM SOLVING

1. JUDGMENT (STRATEGIC) VS. ABILITY TO PROBLEM SOLVE

Judgment (strategic): The tendency to have a balance of traits necessary to discern pertinent information, and formulate an effective strategy. Ability to problem solve: Ability to learn and problem solve.


2. OPTIMISTIC VS. ANALYZES PITFALLS

Optimistic: The tendency to believe the future will be positive. Analyzes Pitfalls: The tendency to scrutinize potential difficulties related to a plan or strategy.


5

PROBLEM SOLVING

3. INNOVATIVE VS. SYSTEMATIC

Innovative: The tendency to create new and more effective ways of doing things. Systematic: The enjoyment of tasks that require carefully or methodically thinking through steps.


4. ANALYTICAL VS. INTUITIVE

Analytical: The tendency to logically examine facts and situations (not necessarily analytical ability). Intuitive: The tendency to use hunches to help make decisions (not necessarily intuitive capabilities).


6

RELATIONSHIP WITH SELF AND OTHERS

1. SELF-IMPROVEMENT VS. SELF-ACCEPTANCE

Self-improvement: The tendency to attempt to develop or better oneself. Self-acceptance: The tendency to like oneself ("I'm O.K. the way I am").


2. HANDLES CONFLICT VS. WARMTH / EMPATHY

Handles Conflict: The tendency to skillfully face discord or strife, and the interpersonal skills necessary to deal with it effectively. Warmth / empathy: The tendency to express positive feelings and affinity toward others.


7

RELATIONSHIP WITH SELF AND OTHERS

3. FRANK VS. DIPLOMATIC

Frank: The tendency to be straightforward, direct, to the point, and forthright. Diplomatic: The tendency to state things in a tactful manner.


4. ASSERTIVE VS. HELPFUL

Assertive: The tendency to put forward personal wants and needs. Helpful: The tendency to respond to others' needs and assist or support others to achieve their goals.
